### 1.1

We are given:

$$
f(t) = Ae^{-|{\frac{t}{\tau}|}}
$$

To compute the amplitude and phase spectra of the signal we use the fourier transform given by:

$$
F(\omega) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}f(t)e^{-j\omega t} ~ dt = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} Ae^{-|{\frac{t}{\tau}|}}e^{-j\omega t}
$$

Let $F(\omega) = I_1 + I_2$ where:

$$
I_1 = A\int_{-\infty}^{0}e^{t/\tau}e^{-j\omega t} ~dt= A\int_{-\infty}^{0}e^{t(\frac{1}{\tau}-jw)} ~dt= A\left[\frac{1}{
\frac{1}{\tau}-j\omega}e^{t(\frac{1}{\tau}-j\omega)}\right]_{-\infty}^{0}= \frac{A}{\frac{1}{\tau}-j\omega}
$$

$$
I_2 = A\int_{0}^{\infty}e^{-t/\tau}e^{-j\omega t} ~dt= A\int_{-\infty}^{0}e^{-t(\frac{1}{\tau}+jw)} ~dt = \frac{A}{\frac{1}{\tau}+j\omega}
$$

This then results in:

$$
F(\omega) = \frac{2A\tau}{1+(\tau\omega)^2}
$$

It can be observed that the signal is real in its entirety and therefore has an argument of 0 for any frequency. The amplitude spectra is visualized below.

### 1.2

$$
E_f = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}|f(t)|^2~dt=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\left(A^2e^{-2|\frac{t}{\tau}|}\right)~dt = A^2\tau
$$

$$
E_F = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}F(\omega)~df = 0.99\cdot E_f
$$

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}|F(\omega)|^2~df = 4A^2\tau^2\int_{-B}^{B} \frac{1}{(1+(2\pi f\tau)^2)^2}~df = 0.99\cdot A^2\tau
$$

Recognizing the form of the integrand, one can set substitute by setting $u = 2\pi f\tau$, thus obtaining:

$$
\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-2\pi\tau B}^{2\pi\tau B}\frac{1}{1+u^2}~du = \frac{0.99}{4}
$$

Evalutaing the integral we get:

$$
\frac{2\pi\tau B}{1+(2\pi\tau B)^2} + \arctan(2\pi\tau B) = 2\pi \frac{0.99}{4}
$$

Solving for B numerically one gets the bandwith by:

$$
\underline{\underline{B \approx 168~kHz}}
$$
